Anu

AI, VR and haptic powered speculative digital legacy platform for digital natives to physically and conversationally interact with their deceased loved ones.

XR Developer Product Manager

Anu is a speculative platform that asks a profoundly human question: as we leave ever-richer digital traces of ourselves, what obligations and possibilities does that create for those we leave behind? The platform allows the living to interact with AI-reconstructed presences of deceased loved ones — not as a simulation of life, but as an archive made conversational and embodied.

The VR environment is designed around presence and intimacy, not fidelity. Rather than pursuing photorealistic avatars, Anu uses impressionistic spatial representations — environments that evoke the personality and places of the deceased through ambient memory cues. The haptic layer (via wearable gloves and a spatial haptic array) allows users to feel proximity and a softened sense of touch, grounding the experience in the body.

As XR developer, I built the Unity-based VR experience including the spatial audio architecture and the conversational AI integration — connecting a fine-tuned language model trained on the digital archive of the deceased to a voiced avatar system. As product manager, I oversaw the ethical framework, user research with participants who had experienced loss, and the interaction design principles governing consent, closure, and healthy use limits.
