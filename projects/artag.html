ARTag — Heads-up Multiplayer AR Laser Tag

This capstone project (CG4002) marked the culmination of my four years of undergraduate computer engineering experiences at NUS, where I collaborated with four teammates to design and build a fully functional wearable AR laser tag system from scratch. Our system explored heads-up, embodied interaction by combining wearable sensing, real-time AI inference, and spatial AR visualization into a seamless multiplayer gameplay experience. Independently, I worked on the development of the heads-up AR game visualizer, conversational AI game tutor and system integration with other parts such as the game evaluation server and motion detecting hardware.

Players competed in 1-on-1 matches while wearing sensor-embedded gloves equipped with IMU and gyroscope modules, along with Microsoft HoloLens 2 headsets for immersive gameplay visuals. Physical gestures such as boxing punches were captured through the wearable sensors and transmitted wirelessly to a neural network model deployed on an FPGA for real-time action recognition. The inference results were then streamed through MQTT to a broker laptop and relayed to the HoloLens 2, where dynamic AR game effects and live status information — such as player health, shield status, and attack outcomes — were visualized directly in the player's first person view. Players can also learn how to play the game in a 3D tutorial room with a conversational AI game tutor prior to the game.
