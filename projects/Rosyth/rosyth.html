Augmented
Rosyth

Architectural design portfolio for a speculative AI- and XR-powered primary school campus — imagining how learning environments transform when intelligence is embedded in space.

Interaction Designer

Augmented Rosyth is a speculative architectural design portfolio reimagining Rosyth Primary School in Singapore as a future campus where AI and XR are woven into the physical infrastructure of learning. The project asks: what does a school look like when space itself becomes a learning interface?

The design explores three spatial paradigms: adaptive classrooms where AR overlays shift based on subject and group learning mode; outdoor discovery trails where the physical campus triggers contextual AI tutors; and a communal "imagination hall" — a large open volume with projection-mapped surfaces that transforms between assembly space, simulation theater, and collaborative design studio.

As interaction designer and product manager, I led the UX framework — defining how students and teachers would navigate the transition between physical and augmented modes — and managed the design sprint cadence across a multidisciplinary team of architects, educators, and technologists. The portfolio was presented as part of a speculative futures brief for urban educational environments.
