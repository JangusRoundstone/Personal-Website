About

Hi! I'm a master student in the Human Centered Design & Engineering (HCDE) program at University of Washington (UW). Through my work, I develop heads-up computing applications and multimodal AI systems, and study how they can augment human perception and cognition for seamless interaction with both the digital and the physical world.

Before UW, I completed my bachelor's degree in computer engineering with first class honours at National University of Singapore (NUS), where I worked on heads-up computing, wearables and human-AI interaction in the NUS-HCI Lab, advised by professor Shengdong Zhao. I also interned at a Singaporean start-up building a Singaporean sign language LLM for a smartphone-based two-way sign language translation application, and co-founded another start-up for mixed-reality based physical education for local secondary school and junior college students in Singapore.

Outside the lab, I play survival horror games like the Resident Evil series, tour distinct architecture in the US like the Seattle Public Library, and think a lot about how long will it take for GUI to become the next CLI.
